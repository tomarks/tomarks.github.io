4-Week Novice Bodyweight Workout Plan

Focus: Upper Body & Hip Strength

Frequency: 3 times per week on non-consecutive days (e.g., Mon/Wed/Fri)

Important Considerations

  • Consult a Doctor: Always consult with your physician before starting any new exercise program.
  • Focus on Form: Perfect form is more important than the number of reps. Watch videos and practice slowly first.
  • Listen to Your Body: Stop if there is sharp pain. Muscle soreness is normal, joint pain is not.
  • Warm-up: Always perform a 5-10 minute warm-up before each workout (e.g., light cardio, arm circles, leg swings, torso twists).
  • Cool-down: Always perform a 5-10 minute cool-down with static stretching after each workout (hold stretches 20-30 seconds).
  • Consistency: Stick to the schedule for best results.
  • Hydration: Drink plenty of water throughout the day.
  • Progression: If an exercise feels too easy, try a slightly harder variation. If too hard, use an easier variation or lower reps.

Exercises

  • Glute Bridges: Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat. Squeeze glutes and lift hips until body forms a straight line from shoulders to knees. Lower slowly.
  • Incline Push-ups: Hands on elevated surface (counter, table), body straight. Lower chest towards surface, elbows slightly tucked, push back up. (Higher surface = easier).
  • Bodyweight Squats: Feet shoulder-width apart. Lower hips back and down like sitting in a chair, keeping chest up and back straight. Push through heels to stand.
  • Plank: Hold body in a straight line from head to heels, supported on forearms and toes (or knees for easier version). Keep core tight, don't let hips sag or rise.
  • Reverse Lunges (Alternating): Step one foot back, lower hips until both knees are ~90 degrees. Front knee over ankle, back knee near floor. Push off back foot to return. Alternate legs. (1 rep = 1 lunge per leg).
  • Bench Dips (using chair/step): Sit on edge, hands gripping edge next to hips. Extend legs (straighter = harder). Push hips forward off edge. Lower body by bending elbows straight back (~90 degrees), push back up.
  • Bird Dog (Alternating): Start on all fours. Extend right arm forward and left leg back simultaneously, keeping back flat and hips level. Return slowly. Repeat on other side. (1 rep = 1 extension per side).

Week 1: Focus on Form & Consistency

Sets per exercise: 2

Rest between sets: 90 seconds

Exercise Reps / Duration
Glute Bridges 10-12 reps
Incline Push-ups 8-10 reps (adjust incline as needed)
Bodyweight Squats 10-12 reps
Plank Hold 20-30 seconds (or Knee Plank)
Reverse Lunges 6-8 reps per leg (12-16 total)
Bench Dips 8-10 reps (adjust leg position as needed)
Bird Dog 6-8 reps per side (12-16 total)

Week 2: Increase Volume

Sets per exercise: 3

Rest between sets: 90 seconds

Exercise Reps / Duration
Glute Bridges 10-12 reps
Incline Push-ups 8-10 reps
Bodyweight Squats 10-12 reps
Plank Hold 30 seconds (try progressing off knees)
Reverse Lunges 6-8 reps per leg (12-16 total)
Bench Dips 8-10 reps
Bird Dog 6-8 reps per side (12-16 total)

Week 3: Increase Intensity

Sets per exercise: 3

Rest between sets: 60-75 seconds

Exercise Reps / Duration
Glute Bridges 12-15 reps
Incline Push-ups 10-12 reps (try lower incline if possible)
Bodyweight Squats 12-15 reps
Plank Hold 30-45 seconds
Reverse Lunges 8-10 reps per leg (16-20 total)
Bench Dips 10-12 reps
Bird Dog 8-10 reps per side (16-20 total)

Week 4: Consolidate Gains

Sets per exercise: 3

Rest between sets: 60 seconds

Exercise Reps / Duration
Glute Bridges 12-15 reps (focus on squeeze)
Incline Push-ups 12-15 reps (or try Knee Push-ups on floor)
Bodyweight Squats 12-15 reps (focus on control)
Plank Hold 45-60 seconds
Reverse Lunges 10-12 reps per leg (20-24 total)
Bench Dips 12-15 reps (try straighter legs if possible)
Bird Dog 10-12 reps per side (20-24 total, focus on stability)

End of Program

After 4 weeks, assess progress. You can repeat this cycle aiming for higher reps or harder variations, or move to a more advanced program. Keep up the great work!
